## A Pointed Question

What happens to brokers in your system?

— Anonymous Student

At the time, I retorted: "They go bankrupt."

### Past Reasoning

TAD3 [came from observing](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/from-trader-trailblazer-web3-stock-investments-sec-review-john-wooten-zvxbc/) our present inefficient, problematic, and centralized capital market. It all started when I realized that an average stock trade goes through 11 middlemen. At the time, I thought that these banks, brokers, and custodians had to be the problem.

#### Thoughts Since

I've grown over the past years building Block Transfer from nothing, speaking with countless successful American businesses, and supporting our first small issuer from South Carolina. Before this startup, the closest thing I had to a "real job" was managing a Subway. That was 7 years ago now.

Over time, I've come to greatly appreciate capitalism's unique ability to efficiently organize society without centralization. Incredibly, the lure of prosperity, when combined with information accessability,[defined my life goal](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6sqCilpMjOE) .

### A Deeper Flaw

Some middlemen in our current stock trading system charge hidden fees, pay exorbitant salaries, and practice unfair business. For instance, academics have shown repeatedly that HFT co-location increases everyday volatility, real spreads, and settlement failures. But stock exchanges keep selling fiber lines to server farms next door for billions, enabled by [misinformation](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UjI7lfTbvrw),[blatant fraud](https://www.google.com/books/edition/Naked_Short_and_Greedy/klnlDwAAQBAJ), and inadequate [education](https://fliphtml5.com/bookcase/kosyg) .
