Coordinated Universal Time ("UTC")

At BlockTrans Syndicate, we rely on UTC as our reference time standard. Our commitment to using UTC ensures consistency and precision in all our operations, ultimately safeguarding your investments.

Basis Date for Legacy Shares

When dealing with restricted stock, we adhere to Rule 144 holding periods. In cases where only a date aquired is known, as generally provided in good faith by issuers, we assume the time aquired to be the next day at 0:00:00 UTC, using this as the base date and time for the legend removal of such shares. Futher, if an aquisition date is nether known nor provided by an investor, we will base the start of the Rule 144 holding period on the date and time we receive a final investor list from clients.

Record Date Policy

We record all investor balances at a ledger closed between 0:00:00 UTC and 0:07:00 UTC (inclusive) on the day after the record date. If you transfer shares from 0:00:00 to 0:07:00 UTC before our record date snapshot executes, then you will not be considered a holder of record on the previous day.

Ex-Dividend Dates

We do not observe ex-dividend dates.

Stock Split Policy

Execution Day Procedures

When it comes to stock splits, we synchronize our processes with UTC to maintain accuracy. We freeze all asset activity the day before a split becomes effective at or about 22:00:00 UTC (strictly after). During the next two hours, we effect the split. We unfreeze accounts the day the split is effective beginning at 0:00:00 UTC (not to exceed 2:00:00 UTC).

No Extended Denial of Transfers

We find these operations in accordance with SEC Rule 17Ad-15 since the account freeze in question (i) does not last longer than three business days, (ii) affects all investors equally, and (iii) notifies investors immediately of transfer requests rejected because of frozen split processing via our app.

History

For transparency and precision, we provide access to past split records at blocktransfer.com/splits/{{assetCode}}.toml, when splits for such asset exist. Asset codes are a union of the issuer's Central Index Key and stock class ("ORD" for ordinary or "PFD" for preferred if no class letter).
